<h3>Privacy and Anonymity Spectrum</h3>

<p><strong>Privacy</strong> involves the control over personal information and the ability to limit access to one's data. It encompasses strategies to safeguard personal information from unauthorized access or exposure.</p>

<p><strong>Anonymity</strong> denotes the state where an individual's identity is not known or traceable by others. It ensures that an individual's actions and interactions cannot be linked back to their real identity.</p>

<p>Privacy and anonymity definitions:</p>

<p><strong>• Identity:</strong> The unique set of characteristics (such as name, address, or biometric data) that can identify an individual.</p>

<p><strong>• Pseudonymity:</strong> A situation where an individual uses a consistent, but non-real name or identifier. This identifier allows for interactions without revealing the person's real identity. According to the <a href="https://alrc.gov.au" target="_blank">Australian Law Reform Commission</a>, pseudonymity is used in various contexts to provide a level of privacy while maintaining some degree of accountability.</p>

<p><strong>• Anonymity:</strong> The state where an individual's identity is completely unknown, with no identifiable markers linking them to their actions. Anonymity is a more secure form of privacy, providing protection against identification and tracking.</p>

<p><strong>• De-Anonymization: </strong> The process or final state of revealing the true identity of a anonymous or pseudonymous person. All data linked to the anonymous or pseudonymous entity can then be connected to the true identity.</p>
